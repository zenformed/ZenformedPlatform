BuildCore is Zenformed's construction CRM — built to help your team manage projects, workflow, documents, payments, and accountability from one place.

This guide introduces the core concepts you'll use every day and points you to the next steps in your setup.

## What BuildCore helps you do

BuildCore connects the full project lifecycle:

- **Manage projects** in a searchable pipeline with filters for stage, priority, and status
- **Coordinate your team** with workflow tasks, assignments, and workflow stages
- **Track payments and budget entries** against project progress
- **Store and share documents** attached to projects and tasks

> **Note:** BuildCore is designed for construction teams of all sizes. You can start with a single project and expand as your organization grows.

## Before you begin

Make sure you have the following ready:

1. A **Zenformed account** with access to BuildCore
2. Your company profile and branding details (optional but recommended)
3. At least one team member to invite through **Teams** (optional but recommended)

> **Tip:** Organization owners and admins can configure **Workflow Settings** before inviting the rest of the team.

## Your first login

Sign in to **Zenformed**, launch **BuildCore** from the apps menu, and you'll land on the **Projects** view — your project pipeline and primary workspace.

```text
Projects list → Open a project → Project information
```

From here, most teams follow this path:

1. Review **Workflow Settings** for your organization
2. Create your first project
3. Invite team members and assign roles from **Teams**
4. Refine workflow stages and templates as your process matures

## Key areas to explore

### Projects

Projects are the center of BuildCore. Each project holds contact details, workflow tasks, documents, payment milestones, budget entries, and accountability records. Parent projects can include **Subprojects** for multi-phase work.

### Reports

Open **Reports** from the sidebar for CRM financial views such as revenue, profit, costs, and receivables.

### Workflow

Customize stages and templates under **Workflow Settings** so your team follows a consistent process from lead to closeout.

> **Note:** Workflow configuration is optional during initial setup. You can use your organization's defaults and refine later.

## Example: project naming

When creating projects, use a consistent naming pattern so your team can find work quickly:

```
[Year] – [Customer Last Name] – [Address or Job Name]
2026 – Martinez – 142 Oak Street Remodel
```

## Need help?

If you run into issues during setup, visit **Troubleshooting** in this help center or contact your organization administrator.
